Foundational datasets

UNHCR manages several important global data sets which are important for nearly any analysis concerning refugees and other forcibly displaced and stateless people. These foundational datasets are the building blocks on which all operational planning is based.

Refugee Data Finder

A database containing information about forcibly displaced populations spanning more than 70 years of statistical activities. It covers displaced populations such as refugees, asylum-seekers and internally displaced people, including their demographics. Stateless people are also included, most of whom have never been displaced.

Learn more

*accessible through Orion

Operational Data Portal

The Operational Data Portal (ODP) enables UNHCR’s institutional responsibility to provide an information and data sharing platform to facilitate coordination of refugee emergencies. This is enabled using independent “situation views” covering major emergencies such as the Sudan situation.

Learn more

GIS Database

UNHCR maintains publicly accessible geo-spatial datasets covering the location of UNHCR offices worldwide, as well as the locations of forcibly displaced and stateless people. This data can be used an any GIS software to create maps and do spatial analysis.

Learn more

*accessible through Orion

Nowcasting Database

Nowcasting - predicting the very recent past or the present - helps to estimate numbers in situations where statistics are not sufficiently timely. In humanitarian and development situations, where collecting up-to-date statistics is challenging, nowcasting can be used to overcome reporting delays.

Learn more

*accessible through Orion (INTERNAL)

Refugee Funding Traker

The Refugee Funding Tracker is designed to help partners involved in inter-agency refugee response to find information on funding for refugee programmes, including within the framework of Refugee Response Plans. The tool shows funding data for refugee response, starting in 2012.
